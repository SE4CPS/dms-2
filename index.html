Research and Teaching Interests

Dr. Berhe has been developing software since 2001 and completed his Ph.D. in Computer Science and Software Engineering in 2011 from the University of Connecticut. His industry responsibility afterward includes developing, coordinating, and leading Industry 4.0 software systems in various domains such as health care, automotive, retail, and e-mobility.

Teaching

Dr. Berhe’s teaching interests include software engineering, database systems, and mobile application development. He is passionate about preparing students with the necessary skills to excel in the rapidly evolving field of technology. His courses are designed to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical experience.

Research

Dr. Berhe’s current research focuses on modeling software ecosystems, particularly in risk assessment for maintenance efforts. His research aims to develop methodologies and tools that help manage the complexities of maintaining and evolving large-scale software systems. His prior research explored secure software engineering in patient-centered clinical ecosystems, emphasizing the process from model to code generation.
